1.2 - All my updated mods

 

ESOUI Mods

 

I have removed the LibStub requirements so they run without it. There is no need for LibStub because the functionality is now built into the game and some authors are simply to stubborn to remove it. Not having embedded libraries and removing LibStub really does help the mods run properly. I had one mod using Custom Compass Pins as an embedded library and when I updated it to the author's latest version it caused an error. Authors for ESO UI mods make the same kinds of mistakes that Nexus mod authors make in regards to optimization of the mods.

1.4 - Chapters, Zone DLC, Dungeon DLC

 

This is up to date as of 04/19/2021

 

To play and be in the guild or do anything you don't need anything but the base game. You will be restricted from doing anything with the chapters Morrowind, Summerset, Elsweyr, Greymoor, Blackwood. If you do not have the chapters you can not access the content for those areas.

 

Chapters

 

Pay close attention to the UESP Wiki section for bundles. For some reason I don't see any information from UESP about the Greymoor and Blackwood bundles at the time of this update. However, if you visit the ESO site for Blackwood since that is the latest chapter then review what you get with the different bundles. If you purchase the upgrade it will cost less but only provides you with the Blackwood chapter.

 

If you are a returning player and you want more for you money then you should purchase either the base Blackwood collection or the Collectors edition since they come with all the previous chapters.

 

Greymoor is the current chapter and Blackwood has not been released at the time this was updated.

 

1.5 - Let's talk Zone and Dungeon DLC.

 

DLC

 

Zone DLC would get you quests, content, story and skill lines. Dungeon DLC provides only Dungeon content which usually has a story and a quest.

1.7 - What to buy

 

ESO Blackwood Edition - Bethesda link

 

I don't know that they offer Morrowind, Summerset, and Elsweyr chapters ALL separately. Unless you have all of the previous chapters, then I recommend the Blackwood Digital Collector's Edition. If you have purchased all of the previous chapters and want to save some money then buy the upgrade instead.

 

Once you have that to have access to all content except dungeons you have two choices.

 

1) Get ESO Plus. It comes with 1650 Crowns (Currency for in game micro purchases) and access to all the Zone and Dungeon DLC. There are other benefits see the ESO Plus section below on this page. 2) Get the Blackwood CE to be up to date with all the chapters then for DLC Guilds and Glory, Dragonhold, Murkmire, and Clockwork City. This means you have all the content with no ESO Plus subscription. You can play any time off and on and never spend money on a subscription you are not using.

 

1.8 - ESO Plus

 

ESO Plus - Bethesda link

 

So you can obviously read all that but from a players perspective what it gives you that is noticeably useful is bank space and bag space. Your bank is doubled in size and all crafting materials go into a virtual bag that is limitless in size. Meaning you loot like crazy and as long as they are crafting mats you will never run out of bag space. Other then that you have to sell it to a vender or break it down into materials for crafting. The other thing that is different is your small house has double the capacity for decorations.

 

Then of course you have access to all the DLC content while ESO Plus is active.
